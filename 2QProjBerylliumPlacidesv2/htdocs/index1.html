A Severe Case of Omofovia

by Joshua Andrew T. Placides


In Greek, omofovia means homophobia: the dislike or prejudice against members of the LGBTQ+ community.

Despite the world traversing into the twenty-first century, homophobia remains rampant in all corners of the world. From America to Asia, to Europe to Africa, hate for homosexuals is still embedded in the cracks of their respective societies – largely due to colonial values brought by colonizers in the 15th century. Certainly, Philippines is no different. Homophobia in the modern-day Philippine society is widespread and even more so due to anti-LGBTQ strongmen such as Manny Pacquiao and the infamous Apollo Quiboloy.

A Severe Case of Omofovia explores the Philippines’ severe case of homophobia from its history to effects on LGBTQ+ Filipinos now.
